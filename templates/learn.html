ü™ô What is Money?

Summary: Money is what we use to buy things we need and want. It comes in coins, bills, and digital forms.

  • Coins: Small metal money (pennies, nickels, dimes)
  • Bills: Paper money (dollar bills)
  • Digital: Numbers in bank accounts or cards

üí° Fun Fact: People once traded shells or animals instead of money!

üê∑ Saving Money

Summary: Saving means keeping money for later instead of spending it all now.

  • Helps buy bigger things later
  • Prepares for emergencies
  • Watching money grow is exciting!

Try This:

Use a clear jar to save coins and watch it fill up!

üõí Needs vs Wants

Summary: Needs are things we must have to live, wants are things we'd like to have.

Needs

  • Food
  • Clothing
  • Shelter

Wants

  • Toys
  • Candy
  • Video games

üí° Tip: Always take care of needs before spending on wants!

üìù Making a Budget

Summary: A budget is a plan for your money to avoid overspending.

  1. Track income (allowance, gifts)
  2. List expenses (savings, spending)
  3. Follow your plan
  4. Adjust as needed

Example Budget:

$20 weekly allowance:
- Save: $8 (40%)
- Spend: $12 (school lunch $5, entertainment $7)

üõç Smart Shopping

Summary: Smart shoppers compare prices and quality before buying.

  • Check unit prices (price per ounce/pound)
  • Look for sales and discounts
  • Read product reviews
  • Avoid impulse buys

üí° Tip: Wait 24 hours before big purchases to avoid impulse buying!

üì± Digital Money

Summary: Digital payments are convenient but require caution.

  • Debit cards spend your money directly
  • Mobile payments (Apple Pay, Venmo)
  • Online purchases need parent approval
  • Always protect passwords/PINs

Security Tip: Never share banking info online!

üéØ Setting Goals

Summary: Financial goals help focus your saving efforts.

  • Short-term: Save for a few weeks/months
  • Long-term: Save for a year or more
  • Make goals SMART: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound

üí≥ Debit vs Credit Cards

Summary: Different cards work differently with your money.

Debit Card

  • Uses your money directly
  • No debt risk
  • Good for daily use

Credit Card

  • Borrowed money
  • Builds credit history
  • Requires discipline

üí° Pro Tip: Pay credit card balances in full each month to avoid interest!

üìä Understanding Taxes

Summary: Taxes fund public services and affect earnings.

  • Income tax: Paid on money earned
  • Sales tax: Added to purchases
  • Paycheck deductions reduce take-home pay
  • Tax returns reconcile payments

Example:

A $15/hr job might pay $12/hr after taxes.

üèÜ Building Credit

Summary: Good credit saves money on loans and rentals.

  • Keep credit card balances low
  • Don't open too many accounts
  • Check credit reports annually

üí° Did You Know? Landlords and employers often check credit history!
